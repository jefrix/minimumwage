The Broken Link: Wages vs. Productivity

Historically, the minimum wage grew alongside worker productivity. Since 1968, that connection has been severed. While productivity has soared, the real value of the minimum wage has plummeted.

Poverty Wage vs. Living Wage

A full-time job at the federal minimum wage is not enough to live on. It falls short of the federal poverty line for a single adult and is dwarfed by the estimated national living wage needed to cover basic expenses.

Who Earns the Minimum Wage?

Disproportionately Young Workers

While making up only one-fifth of hourly workers, those under 25 account for nearly half of all minimum wage earners.

Concentrated in Service Industries

The vast majority of minimum wage workers are in service roles, especially food preparation and serving.

A Tale of 50 States: The Cost of Living Divide

A single federal wage cannot account for the vast differences in living costs across the country. A wage that is barely adequate in one state is a poverty wage in another.
